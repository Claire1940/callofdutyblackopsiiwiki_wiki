<Section icon={Map} title="Map Overview and Lore Integration">

**Call of Duty Black Ops II Nuketown Zombies** takes players back to the iconic Nevada testing site, but with a post-apocalyptic twist. Set during the events of the "Moon" map from the original Black Ops, this location serves as a bridge between the classic era and the futuristic setting of 2025. Following a nuclear explosion triggered by a Broken Arrow drill, radiation scientists are turned into the undead by Element 115. Players take control of anonymous CIA or CDC agents sent to investigate the distress signal, only to become stranded in the radioactive ruins.

The map is a direct reimagining of the Nuketown multiplayer map, featuring the familiar Green and Yellow houses, the central cul-de-sac, and the bunker where Marlton Johnson is famously hiding. Unlike other maps in the Black Ops II Zombies suite, Nuketown focuses purely on survival, stripping away complex Easter Egg quests in favor of high-intensity, round-based combat.

<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics and Randomization">

The defining feature of **Call of Duty Black Ops II Nuketown Zombies** is the randomization of Perk-a-Cola machines and the Pack-a-Punch machine. Unlike traditional maps where these are in fixed locations, here they fall from the sky at random intervals, usually accompanied by a nuclear siren. This forces players to adapt their strategy based on which Perk arrives first. If Juggernog drops in round 20 instead of round 4, your survival tactics must shift toward extreme evasion.

Another unique aspect is the removal of boardable windows. In Nuketown, zombies climb over fences, drop from the sky, and crawl out of the ground. This eliminates the "window camping" strategy common in maps like Kino der Toten and necessitates a constant "kiting" or "training" movement pattern around the central map area.

<Callout type="warning" title="Survival Warning">
Because there are no windows to repair, the Carpenter power-up does not exist on this map. Do not rely on mid-round breaks to reset your defenses.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Perk-a-Cola and Pack-a-Punch Strategy">

Since you cannot predict when Juggernog or Speed Cola will arrive, you must master the layout of the potential landing zones. There are four primary landing spots for the machines: the backyard of the Green House, the backyard of the Yellow House, the garage of the Green House, and the spawn area.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor the Siren">
    Listen for the nuclear air raid siren. This indicates a machine is about to crash-land. Look at the sky to track the meteor's path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Landing Zone">
    If a machine lands in a tight area like the Green House garage, clear the zombies immediately. Getting trapped while buying a perk is the #1 cause of death in Nuketown.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Juggernog">
    Once Juggernog lands, save all points to acquire it. Without it, the narrow paths between the houses are death traps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Pack-a-Punch Timing">
    Wait to Pack-a-Punch until you have a weapon like the Galil or RPD. The machine is often in a dangerous spot, so don't risk it for low-tier weapons.
  </Step>
</Steps>
