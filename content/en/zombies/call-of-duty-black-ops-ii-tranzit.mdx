<Section icon={Map} title="Map Overview and Primary Locations">

The **call of duty black ops ii tranzit** experience, also known as Green Run, is an expansive post-apocalyptic map that revolutionized the Zombies mode. Unlike traditional maps, TranZit is divided into several major "islands" of safety connected by a fog-filled wasteland. Survival depends on understanding the unique layout of these locations and the resources they provide.

| Location | Key Perk | Unique Resource | Strategy Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bus Depot** | Quick Revive | Turbine Parts | Starting area, move out fast. |
| **Diner** | Speed Cola | Galvaknuckles | Best for early wall-buys and shield. |
| **Farm** | Double Tap | Mystery Box | Tight quarters, high risk training. |
| **Power Station** | Tombstone | Power Switch | Linear path, watch the bottomless pit. |
| **Town** | Juggernog | Bank & Pack-a-Punch | The ultimate hub for late-game setup. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Starting Strategy">
In the Bus Depot, do not buy the M14 or Olympia immediately. Instead, build the Turbine and use it to open the side door for free. This saves points for the MP5 at the Diner stop.
</Callout>

The map also features several "Off Route" locations hidden within the fog. These include the **Tunnel** (between Depot and Diner), the **Hunter's Cabin** (between Power and Town), and the legendary **Nacht der Untoten** prototype hidden in the cornfields. These areas are essential for finding parts for the Thrustodyne Aeronautics Model 23, the map's unique Wonder Weapon.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Bus and Portal Navigation">

The Bus is your primary lifeline in **call of duty black ops ii tranzit**, driven by the robotic T.E.D.D. While the bus follows a set path, you can enhance its utility by finding and attaching three specific upgrades found in random tool sheds across the map.

| Bus Upgrade | Effect | Installation Point |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cow Catcher** | Instantly kills zombies in the bus path | Front of the bus |
| **Ladder** | Allows access to the roof from outside | Rear left side |
| **Roof Hatch** | Allows access to the roof from inside | Ceiling of the bus |

<Callout type="warning" title="T.E.D.D. Behavior">
Do not attack the bus driver. If T.E.D.D. takes too much damage from players, he will lock the doors and attempt to kick everyone off, or even drive away prematurely, leaving you stranded in the fog.
</Callout>

If you miss the bus, you must use the **Portal** system to navigate. This is a high-risk, high-reward method of fast travel that requires a specific interaction with the environment and the Denizens.

**How to Activate Portals:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate a Lamp Post">
    Find one of the green-glowing lamp posts located at major stops or halfway points in the fog.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lure a Denizen">
    Step into the fog and wait for a Denizen to spawn and chase you toward the lamp post.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Create the Portal">
    Stand directly under the lit lamp post. The Denizen will jump off you and dig a hole in the ground, creating a green portal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Jump In">
    Jump into the hole to be teleported to a random active lamp post elsewhere on the map.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Buildables and Power Management">

TranZit introduced the "Buildable" system, where players collect parts to create powerful tools. The most important early-game item is the **Turbine**, which acts as a portable power source. Later, you can construct the **Thrustodyne Aeronautics Model 23** (Jet Gun), though it is notorious for breaking if overused.

<Callout type="success" title="Jet Gun Management">
To prevent the Jet Gun from exploding, watch the needle on the pressure gauge. If it reaches the red zone, stop firing immediately. If it breaks, the parts scatter and must be picked up again.
</Callout>

In addition to offensive tools, you can build defensive items like the **Zombie Shield** (Diner) and the **Electric Trap** (Power Station). These are vital for high-round strategies where the bus is no longer a viable place to stay.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Enemies and Combat Tactics">

The **call of duty black ops ii tranzit** environment introduces enemies that require specific tactics. Beyond standard zombies, you will face the **Denizens of the Forest**, the elusive **Avogadro**, and flaming zombies created by the lava cracks.

| Enemy Type | Weakness | Threat Level | Combat Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Denizen** | Melee / Galvaknuckles | Medium | Look up and melee when they scream. |
| **Avogadro** | EMP Grenades / Melee | High | Only damageable in humanoid form. |
| **Fire Zombie** | Distance / Headshots | Low | They explode on death; do not melee. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avogadro Alert">
The Avogadro spawns only after the power is turned on. He can disable the bus and your electronic equipment. If you don't have EMP grenades, use Galvaknuckles to kill him in two hits.
</Callout>

The fog itself is an enemy. It slows you down and spawns Denizens infinitely. Never stay in the fog without a Bowie Knife or Galvaknuckles, as the standard knife takes five hits to kill a single Denizen, often leading to a quick death if multiple spawn.

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression and Global Systems">

One of the most unique features of **call of duty black ops ii tranzit** is the persistent progression system. The **Bank** and **Weapon Locker** allow you to carry over resources from one match to another, making early-game setups significantly easier in 2026 playstyles.

**The Bank System:**
Located in the Town, the Bank vault can be opened with explosives. Inside, you can deposit points in increments of 1,000. These points stay in your "account" across different games and even different maps like Die Rise and Buried. You can also share points with teammates by using Galvaknuckles on the keys in the vault.

**Persistent Upgrades (Perma-Perks):**
These are hidden buffs earned by performing specific actions. For example, reviving teammates multiple times in a single game can earn you "Quick Revive Pro," which persists until you fail to revive someone.
