<Section icon={Shield} title="Core Survival: Power, Elevators, and Perk Management">

In **Call of Duty Black Ops II Die Rise**, the first priority is restoring power to the skyscrapers. This activates the elevators, which carry both the Perk-a-Cola machines and the Pack-a-Punch machine. Because the machines move randomly between floors, players must constantly monitor elevator shafts to find essential upgrades like Jugger-Nog or Speed Cola.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Restoring the Power">
    From the spawn room, drop down through the elevator shafts or use the stairs to reach the lower maintenance levels. Locate the power lever in the main generator room. Flipping this switch activates all elevators and enables the use of Perk machines.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mastering Elevator Timing">
    Elevators move independently. If a Perk machine is on a different floor, wait by the shaft. When the elevator arrives, you can purchase the Perk through the gate or jump on top of the elevator to travel to other floors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locating the Pack-a-Punch">
    The Pack-a-Punch machine is located inside one of the elevators on the rooftop area. It cycles through different levels just like the Perk machines. If it disappears, you must wait for it to return to your current floor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Perk Machine | Base Cost | Function | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Jugger-Nog** | 2500 Points | Increases health from 2 to 5 hits | Priority #1; find it immediately after power. |
| **Quick Revive** | 500/1500 | Self-revive (Solo) or faster revive | Essential for solo play on the rooftops. |
| **Who's Who** | 2000 Points | Spawns a doppelganger upon death | High risk; use only if you know the map layout. |
| **Mule Kick** | 4000 Points | Allows carrying a third weapon | Great for holding the Sliquifier and two wall guns. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Elevator Camping">
If you are trapped in an elevator with a horde outside, wait for it to move. The elevator acts as a temporary safe zone, but be careful not to get crushed against the ceiling or floor transitions.
</Callout>

It fires a purple goo that makes floors slippery and creates a chain-reaction explosion that can wipe out an entire round's worth of zombies with a single shot. Additionally, the **Trample Steam** is a required buildable for navigating the map and setting up traps.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapon Tier List: Best Loadouts for Die Rise">

Choosing the right arsenal is critical in **Call of Duty Black Ops II Die Rise** because of the limited space. Large, heavy weapons like LMGs can slow you down during jumps, while SMGs provide the mobility needed to outrun the "Minion" rounds.

| Tier | Weapon Name | Class | Why It Wins |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S** | **Sliquifier** | Wonder Weapon | Best crowd control and infinite damage. |
| **S** | **AN-94** | Assault Rifle | High damage and accessible wall-buy in the cafeteria. |
| **A** | **SVU-AS** | Sniper Rifle | Semi-auto with high headshot multiplier; great for hallways. |
| **A** | **PDW-57** | SMG | Massive magazine size for a wall gun; found near the spawn. |
| **B** | **Ray Gun MK II** | Wonder Weapon | High burst damage; requires Vengeance DLC/Season Pass. |
| **B** | **Galvaknuckles** | Melee | One-hit kill on Minions; essential for earning free Perks. |

**Recommended Loadout for High Rounds:**
1. **Primary**: Sliquifier (for clearing hordes).
2. **Secondary**: AN-94 (Pack-a-Punched as the "Actuated Neutralizer 9400") for point farming.
3. **Melee**: Galvaknuckles for the special Minion rounds.

<Callout type="info" title="The Minion Rounds">
Every 5-7 rounds, Jumping Minions will replace standard zombies. If you kill all Minions without missing a shot or using only melee (Galvaknuckles), you will receive a free Perk bottle and a Max Ammo.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Easter Egg Guide: High Maintenance">

The main Easter Egg in Die Rise, titled **High Maintenance**, involves a conflict between Dr. Richtofen and Maxis. Completing this quest requires a coordinated four-player team. Below is the checklist and primary steps for the Maxis path, which is generally preferred by players for its rewards.

<Checklist
  id="die-rise-ee-prep"
  title="Easter Egg Prerequisites:"
  items={[
    "Four players with coordinated communication.",
    "The Sliquifier built and equipped by one player.",
    "All players have a Trample Steam built.",
    "Galvaknuckles purchased by at least two players.",
    "Power must be turned ON."
  ]}
/>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Elevator Symbols">
    All four players must stand on the glowing golden symbols located on top of the four main elevators simultaneously. This triggers the first step of the quest.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Floor Symbols">
    Locate the four symbols on the floors of the skyscrapers. Players must step on them in a specific order that changes every game. Trial and error is required; if a symbol glows, it is the correct one in the sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Sniper Tower">
    Use the Sliquifier to coat the balls under the lion statues on the rooftop. Then, use the Trample Steams to fling zombies into the tower. This charges the tower with energy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Final Step">
    Players must use the Galvaknuckles to hit the four corners of the transmission tower in a specific order. Once successful, the Easter Egg is complete, and players receive all Perks permanently for the duration of the match.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Maxis vs. Richtofen">
The Maxis path requires the Trample Steam and ballistic weapons, while the Richtofen path requires the Sliquifier and specifically the SVU-AS or DSR-50. Choose your path based on your team's preferred weapon loadout.
</Callout>
