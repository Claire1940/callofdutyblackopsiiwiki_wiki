<Section icon={Map} title="Map Overview and Core Mechanics">

**Call of Duty Black Ops II Buried**, also known as Resolution 1295, represents one of the most mechanically dense experiences in the Zombies franchise. Released as part of the Vengeance DLC, it places players in an underground Western town located in Angola. Chronologically, it serves as the final chapter for the Victis crew—Misty, Marlton, Russman, and Stuhlinger—in the original timeline. The map is famous for its verticality, narrow corridors, and the introduction of Arthur, a friendly non-playable character who can drastically alter the game's difficulty.

Unlike previous maps that focused on open-field survival, Buried emphasizes environmental manipulation. Players must utilize the Bank and Weapon Locker systems to carry over resources from previous games like TranZit and Die Rise. This interconnected economy allows for a "power start," where players can potentially reach full power within the first five rounds.

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Setting** | Underground Western Town (Angola) |
| **Playable Crew** | Victis (Misty, Marlton, Russman, Stuhlinger) |
| **New Perk** | Vulture Aid Elixir |
| **Wonder Weapon** | The Paralyzer / Ray Gun Mark II |
| **Special NPC** | Arthur (The Giant) |
| **Main Objective** | Mined Games Easter Egg (Maxis vs. Richtofen) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Start">
Use the **Bank system** early. If you have stored points from previous sessions, you can withdraw them immediately at the start of the match to buy all perks and the LSAT off the wall before Round 2 even begins.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Arthur the Giant: Candy and Booze Guide">

Arthur is the centerpiece of **call of duty black ops ii buried**. Found locked in a cell near the Courthouse, he requires a Key to be released. Once free, his behavior is dictated by two items: Booze and Candy. Managing Arthur correctly is the difference between a struggling run and an optimized high-round setup.

Arthur can perform a variety of tasks, from destroying barricades to locking the Mystery Box in place. Using Candy near a buildable bench will even cause him to find and assemble the parts automatically, saving the player significant time and risk.

| Item | Action Triggered | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Booze** | Charge/Destroy | Used to break debris blocking the Church, Mansion, and Jugger-Nog. |
| **Candy** | Combat Support | Arthur will aggressively attack zombies for a short duration. |
| **Candy (at Bench)** | Auto-Build | Causes Arthur to find all parts for a buildable and assemble them instantly. |
| **Candy (at Box)** | Box Lock | Prevents the Mystery Box from moving to a new location for the rest of the game. |
| **Candy (at Crawler)** | Item Fetch | Arthur will pick up a crawler and keep it safe, preventing the round from ending. |

<Callout type="success" title="Arthur Combat Tip">
If you feed Arthur Candy while he is facing a zombie, he will go into a rage mode. In this state, he is effectively invincible and will clear out entire hordes, which is perfect for revining downed teammates in tight spaces.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Buildables and The Ghost Mansion">

The buildable system returns with four primary traps and utilities. The **Subsurface Resonator** is the most powerful, emitting sonic waves that pulverize zombies. However, it requires a **Turbine** to power it. Positioning these correctly in the narrow hallways of the town creates an impenetrable defense for high-round camping.

**The Ghost Mansion** is a high-risk, high-reward zone. A ghostly woman haunts the halls; if she touches you, she steals 2,000 points. If you have no points, she will damage you instead. Successfully navigating the mansion and killing all ghosts rewards the team with a Free Perk Bottle.

| Buildable Item | Components | Best Placement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Subsurface Resonator** | Board, Dish, Motor, Wire | Top of the Saloon or inside the Courthouse. |
| **Head Chopper** | Blade, Motor, Wire, Board | Directly above doorways or narrow stairs. |
| **Trample Steam** | Bellows, Compressor, Screen, Flag | Gaps between buildings for quick traversal. |
| **Turbine** | Fan, Tail, Engine | Must be placed behind the Resonator to power it. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Mansion Warning">
Do not enter the Mansion with a large sum of points unless you are confident in your aim. The Ghosts spawn in proportion to the number of players inside. If you lose all your points, your survival chances drop significantly as you won't be able to buy ammo or perks.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Pack-a-Punch Guide">

Unlocking the Pack-a-Punch machine in **call of duty black ops ii buried** requires navigating the Hedge Maze located behind the Ghost Mansion. Unlike other maps where you flip a switch, here you must survive the "point-drain" zone and navigate a shifting labyrinth.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Release Arthur">
    Find the Cell Key (usually near the cell or in the Courthouse) and let Arthur out. Feed him Booze to break the debris blocking the path to the Mansion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Traverse the Mansion">
    Enter the Mansion and kill the Ghosts. Ensure you have at least 2,000 points so you don't take damage if hit, but try to stay at range to keep your cash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Navigate the Hedge Maze">
    Exit the back of the Mansion into the maze. The maze configuration changes every 10 seconds. Look for the gazebo in the center.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Locate the Machine">
    Drop down the spiral staircase inside the gazebo. The Pack-a-Punch machine is located at the bottom in the "Processing" area.
  </Step>
</Steps>
