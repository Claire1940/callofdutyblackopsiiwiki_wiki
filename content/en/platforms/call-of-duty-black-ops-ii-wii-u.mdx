<Section icon={Shield} title="Multiplayer and Local Co-op Optimization">

The multiplayer experience in **call of duty black ops ii wii u** benefits significantly from the dual-screen setup, particularly in local co-op modes. Traditionally, split-screen gaming requires players to share a single TV, cutting their field of view in half and often leading to "screen peeking."

On the Wii U, this issue is solved. One player can use the TV screen in full resolution while the second player uses the GamePad screen. This provides both players with a private, full-screen experience, which is particularly useful in 1v1 matches or when playing the popular Zombies mode.

| Mode | Player 1 Screen | Player 2 Screen | Screen Peeking? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Split-Screen** | Top Half TV | Bottom Half TV | Yes |
| **Wii U Dual-Screen** | Full TV | GamePad Screen | No |
| **Zombies Co-op** | Full TV | GamePad Screen | No |

</Callout>
