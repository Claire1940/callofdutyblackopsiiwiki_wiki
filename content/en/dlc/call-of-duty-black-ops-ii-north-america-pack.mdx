<Section icon={Target} title="Multiplayer Matchmaking and Network Optimization">

To find active lobbies quickly in the North American region, your network settings must be optimized. Because the game relies on a hybrid server and peer-to-peer system, your Network Address Translation (NAT) type determines how easily you can connect to other players.

Having a restricted NAT type will limit your matchmaking pool, resulting in long search times or connection failures.

| NAT Type | Matchmaking Speed | Player Connectivity | Recommended Actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open | Fast | Connects to all players | None. Ideal configuration. |
| Moderate | Moderate | Connects to Open/Moderate | Enable UPnP on router. |
| Strict | Slow | Connects to Open only | Forward ports 3074 (UDP/TCP). |

To achieve an Open NAT status, you may need to access your router settings and configure port forwarding rules specifically for Xbox Live and Activision servers.

| Protocol | Port Number | Traffic Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| TCP | 3074 | Game Data | Xbox Live Connectivity |
| UDP | 3074 | Voice & Matchmaking | Peer-to-Peer Connection |
| UDP | 88 | System Data | Xbox Live Core Services |
| UDP | 500 | Security | IPsec Tunneling |

<Callout type="success" title="Instant Open NAT Method">
Setting your router's DMZ to your console's static IP address is a quick way to achieve an Open NAT type instantly.
</Callout>
