### Detailed Map Breakdowns

**Cove** is set on a small, forgotten island in the Indian Ocean. The central feature is a crashed smuggling plane which serves as the primary chokepoint. Because the map is circular with very little hard cover on the outskirts, players should focus on the "power positions" within the wreckage. Shotguns and SMGs dominate the interior of the plane, while Assault Rifles are best for patrolling the rocky perimeter.

**Detour** offers a unique longitudinal layout. Set on a collapsed suspension bridge in the East Coast, the map is divided into two main levels: the top deck and the lower maintenance walkways. Snipers will find plenty of opportunities on the top deck, but the real movement happens below. If you find yourself pinned down by long-range fire, use the lower pipes and scaffolding to flank the enemy team.

**Rush** is arguably the most popular map in the pack for casual play. It is a paintball course turned into a lethal arena. The map is divided into several "fields" with inflatable cover and wooden structures. Movement speed is key here. Using the "Dexterity" and "Extreme Conditioning" perks allows you to navigate the obstacles faster than your opponents, making it the ideal map for high-score streaks.

**Uplink** adapts the layout of Summit into a futuristic rain-soaked facility. The central control room remains the most important area to control. Unlike the original Summit, the aesthetic changes in Uplink provide better visibility in certain areas, though the rain can occasionally obscure long-distance targets.

**Buried** is the centerpiece of the **call of duty black ops ii vengeance dlc** for Zombies fans. It transports the four protagonists to a subterranean Old West town that has been swallowed by the earth. This map introduced several revolutionary mechanics to the Zombies franchise, including a friendly giant NPC and the highly sought-after Paralyzer wonder weapon.

| Feature | Function | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

Buried is often considered one of the easier maps for high-round attempts due to the sheer power of the buildables and the Paralyzer. However, navigating the town requires careful planning, especially when dealing with the "Ghosts" in the mansion who can steal your points upon contact.

<Callout type="tip" title="Arthur (The Giant) Management">
Always keep a steady supply of Booze and Candy. Use Booze to make Arthur smash through barricades and debris, opening up the map without spending points. Use Candy near a crafting table to make him build items for you instantly, or near a crawler to make him hold it indefinitely while you set up your easter egg steps.
</Callout>

### Survival Strategies for Buried

To survive past round 30 in Buried, players must master the verticality of the map. The Paralyzer allows players to "fly" by aiming at the ground and jumping, which provides access to rooftops that are otherwise unreachable. This is the primary method for avoiding large hordes in the narrow streets of the town.

The Bank system, carried over from Die Rise and Tranzit, is also present here. Players can store points in the bank to be retrieved in later games, making the initial setup phase of Buried significantly faster than other maps. If you are playing in 2026, ensure your bank is maxed out at 250,000 points to allow for an immediate Pack-a-Punch on Round 1.

### The Paralyzer and Time Bomb

In addition to the Ray Gun Mark II, Buried introduces two unique items:
1.  **The Paralyzer**: A wonder weapon with infinite ammo that operates on a cooling mechanic. It slows zombies down until they eventually disintegrate. It also allows the player to hover and reach rooftops.
2.  **The Time Bomb**: A tactical equipment that allows players to "save" the current state of the game. If the team is about to go down or a mistake is made during an Easter Egg step, the player can detonate the bomb to revert the game back to the exact moment it was placed (including points, perks, and round number).

### Objective Play on Uplink

When playing Domination or Hardpoint on Uplink, the "B" flag (or the central Hardpoint) is notoriously difficult to hold because it is overlooked by the central window. 

| Objective Position | Risk Level | Recommended Equipment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Central Control Room** | **Very High** | Trophy System, Flak Jacket |
| **Server Room** | Medium | Tactical Mask, Shock Charge |
| **Outside Balcony** | High | Smoke Grenade, Target Finder |

Always use a **Trophy System** when capturing the central point on Uplink. The verticality of the map allows enemies to rain down grenades from the upper walkways, and a Trophy System is the only way to survive a sustained assault.
