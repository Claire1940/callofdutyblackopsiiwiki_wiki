<Section icon={Book} title="Beginner Guide: Getting Started in 2026">

Whether you are a returning veteran or a new recruit joining via the PS5 launch, understanding the three core pillars of **Call of Duty Black Ops II** is essential. The game is unique for its non-linear campaign and the introduction of the Pick 10 system in multiplayer.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose Your Entry Point">
    Start with the **Campaign** on Recruit or Regular difficulty to familiarize yourself with the near-future gadgets and weapon handling. If you prefer cooperative play, jump into **Zombies (Town Survival)** for a simplified survival experience.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Game Settings">
    Adjust your **Look Sensitivity**. Most professional players recommend a setting between 4 and 6 for a balance of precision and turning speed. Ensure your **Brightness** is calibrated so you can spot enemies in darker maps like Plaza.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Pick 10 System">
    In Multiplayer, you have 10 points to spend on your loadout. Every weapon, attachment, perk, and piece of equipment costs 1 point. Don't feel obligated to fill every slot; sometimes, an extra perk is more valuable than a secondary weapon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn Map Flow">
    Focus on "Three-Lane" maps like **Raid** or **Standoff**. These maps have a predictable flow, making it easier to anticipate where enemies will spawn and which lanes are currently being contested.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Zombies Point Management">
In early Zombies rounds, use your knife or a pistol to maximize point gain. Avoid buying the first wall weapon you see; save your points to open doors and reach the **Jugger-Nog** machine as quickly as possible.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Campaign Walkthrough and Endings">

The campaign of **Call of Duty Black Ops II** features a branching narrative where your decisions in 1986 and 2025 determine the fate of the world. There are several major endings, ranging from a global uprising to a peaceful resolution of the Second Cold War.

### Critical Story Choices
| Mission | Character | Decision/Action | Long-term Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Old Wounds** | Alex Mason | Resist the Numbers | Reveals CIA mole early |
| **Karma** | David Mason | Kill DeFalco | Saves Chloe Lynch (Karma) |
| **Suffer With Me** | Frank Woods | Shoot target in legs | **Alex Mason survives** to 2025 |
| **Achilles' Veil** | Farid | Shoot Harper | Farid survives to save Karma |
| **Judgment Day** | David Mason | Capture Menendez | Produces the "Best" ending |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strike Force Missions">
Do not ignore the **Strike Force** missions. Successfully completing all of them (FOB Spectre, Shipwreck, I.E.D., and Dispatch) is required to secure the alliance between the US and China, which is necessary for the best possible ending.
</Callout>

### How to Get the Best Ending
1. **Save Alex Mason**: Shoot him in the legs during "Suffer With Me."
2. **Save Karma**: Kill DeFalco in "Karma" or rescue her in the "Second Chance" Strike Force mission.
3. **Keep Farid Alive**: Choose to shoot Harper in "Achilles' Veil."
4. **Spare Admiral Briggs**: Shoot him in the leg during "Odysseus" to keep the USS Barack Obama functional.
5. **Capture Menendez**: Do not execute him in the final mission "Judgment Day."
