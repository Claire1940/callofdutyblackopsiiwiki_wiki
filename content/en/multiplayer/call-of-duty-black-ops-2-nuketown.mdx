<Section icon={Map} title="Overview of Call of Duty Black Ops 2 Nuketown Zombies">

**Call of Duty Black Ops 2 Nuketown** Zombies is a unique survival experience set in the aftermath of a nuclear test. Unlike traditional maps with fixed perk locations, Nuketown introduces a vertical element of randomness. As a radiation-scarred version of the iconic multiplayer map, it provides a tight, frantic environment that rewards players who can adapt to shifting resources.

The map is canonically set on October 13th, 2025. Following a Broken Arrow drill operation that struck Element 115, a nuclear explosion leveled the site. Players take control of anonymous CIA or CDC agents investigating the ruins. Throughout the match, you can hear the progression of Richtofen’s Grand Scheme from the Moon map, which is reflected in the changing eye color of the zombies from yellow to blue.

<Section icon={Target} title="Map Layout and Key Locations">

Navigating **Call of Duty Black Ops 2 Nuketown** requires a deep understanding of the two primary houses and the central "no man's land." Debris blocks many paths, and the tight corridors of the houses make them dangerous for high-round camping.

| Location | Key Features | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yellow House** | Shed power-up, M16A1 wall buy | High risk, contains the backyard shed with rotating power-ups. |
| **Green House** | Garage perk spawn, AK74u wall buy | Good for early points; the garage is a common Pack-a-Punch drop spot. |
| **Central Area** | Red truck, Nuketown sign, Clock | Best area for training zombies in an "8" pattern around the buses. |
| **Bunker** | Marlton Johnson's hiding spot | Purely for lore and Easter Eggs; cannot be entered by players. |

The Nuketown sign in the center functions as a kill counter. Every time the counter hits a multiple of 100, the clock hand moves. This clock governs the timing of the random Perk and Pack-a-Punch drops that fall from the sky.

<Callout type="warning" title="Garage Danger">
The garage in the Green House is a very tight space. If a Perk machine or the Pack-a-Punch lands here mid-round, do not attempt to use it without a Zombie Shield or a distraction like Monkey Bombs.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Zap} title="The Random Perk & Pack-a-Punch System">

Survival in **Call of Duty Black Ops 2 Nuketown** is heavily dependent on the order in which perks arrive. Because the machines fall at random intervals, a game where Juggernog arrives at Round 20 is significantly harder than one where it arrives at Round 4.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Survival & Point Accumulation">
    Focus on wall buys like the M14 or Olympia to build points quickly. Do not open the houses until you have at least 3,000 points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Listen for the Siren">
    A nuclear alarm siren will sound when a machine is about to fall. Look at the sky to see where the meteor is landing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear Landing Zones">
    If a machine lands behind a debris pile, you must pay to clear that debris before you can access the Perk or Pack-a-Punch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Activate the Clock">
    Kills on the map increment the sign counter. Every 100 kills accelerates the arrival of the next machine.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Perk Machine | Cost | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Juggernog** | 2500 | Increases health from 2 hits to 5 hits. |
| **Quick Revive** | 500/1500 | Self-revive in Solo; faster teammate revive in Co-op. |
| **Speed Cola** | 3000 | Cuts reload time in half and speeds up barrier repairs. |
| **Double Tap II** | 2000 | Increases fire rate and doubles bullet damage output. |
| **Pack-a-Punch** | 5000 | Upgrades weapons with higher damage and unique attachments. |

<Callout type="info" title="No Special Rounds">
Nuketown Zombies does not have "Hellhound" or "Max Ammo" rounds. You must rely on random drops from zombies or the shed power-up to replenish ammunition.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weaponry and Wall Buys">

Choosing the right loadout in **Call of Duty Black Ops 2 Nuketown** is vital because of the map's small size. Shotguns and high-mobility SMGs generally perform better than heavy LMGs, which can slow you down in the tight backyards.

### Essential Wall Buys
| Weapon | Cost | Location | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B23R** | 1000 | Side of the bus | Excellent early-round point builder. |
| **MP5** | 1000 | Inside Yellow House | Reliable mid-round weapon with easy ammo access. |
| **AK74u** | 1200 | Inside Green House | High damage SMG; great for training in the center. |
| **Galvaknuckles**| 6000 | Inside Red Truck | One-hit kill until round 14; essential for high rounds. |
| **Bowie Knife** | 3000 | Backyard fence | Great for points if Juggernog drops early. |

### Mystery Box Highlights
The Mystery Box in Nuketown contains the standard Black Ops 2 arsenal, but the addition of the **Ray Gun Mark II** (requires Vengeance DLC) changed the meta for this map.

- **Ray Gun Mark II**: The best weapon for high rounds due to its burst fire and high penetration.
- **Monkey Bombs**: Critical for late-game revives or accessing the Pack-a-Punch in tight spots.
- **Galil / RPD**: High capacity weapons that are best used once you have Speed Cola.

<Callout type="success" title="Galvaknuckles Strategy">
The Red Truck in the center costs 3,000 points to open. Inside, you can buy the Galvaknuckles for 6,000. While expensive, they allow you to punch through zombies easily and are the most effective melee tool available on the map.
</Callout>
