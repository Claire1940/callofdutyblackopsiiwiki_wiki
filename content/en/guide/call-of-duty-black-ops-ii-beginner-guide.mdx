<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Beginner Progression">

Starting a legendary shooter can feel overwhelming. Follow these structured steps to configure your settings, learn the movement systems, and complete your very first matches successfully.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Configure Controls & Audio">
    Before entering combat, adjust your settings. Start with a low-to-medium look sensitivity (horizontal and vertical 4 or 5) and raise it gradually as your target tracking improves. Set your brightness so dark corners remain visible without washing out bright outdoor areas. Use headphones to pinpoint enemy footsteps and distant gunfire.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Play the Campaign on Recruit">
    Use the single-player Campaign to practice basic movement, aiming down sights, reloading, crouching, and throwing equipment. Recruit difficulty allows you to make mistakes without immediate punishment, giving you room to master weapon handling and vehicle controls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build a Simple Pick 10 Class">
    Once you reach Level 4 in Multiplayer, you unlock Create-a-Class. You have exactly 10 allocation points to spend. Keep your first loadout simple: select a reliable primary weapon, one or two attachments, three perks, and a lethal piece of equipment. Avoid complex Wildcards until you understand the basic gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn the Zombies Point Economy">
    In Zombies, do not spend points immediately. Use the early rounds to earn points by shooting zombies in the leg and finishing them with a melee attack. Buy a dependable wall weapon (like the MP5 or M14) before opening doors, and prioritize buying the Jugger-Nog perk as soon as you find it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Settings Calibration">
Always test new control sensitivity settings for at least two full matches before making further adjustments. Changing your sensitivity too frequently will disrupt your muscle memory.
</Callout>

</Section>
