<Section icon={Target} title="How to Configure Your Best Settings">

Finding your ideal look sensitivity and button configuration requires testing under low-pressure conditions. Use the single-player Campaign on Recruit difficulty or a private local match to adjust your settings before stepping into competitive multiplayer lobbies.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set Look Sensitivity">
    Begin with a low-to-medium look sensitivity (typically 3 to 5 on consoles). Move your camera around rapidly. If you constantly over-correct your aim, lower the sensitivity by one increment and test again.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Button Layout Preset">
    Navigate to the options menu and choose a layout that fits your grip. If you prefer jumping during gunfights, look into claw grips or alternative bumper configurations to keep your right thumb on the aiming stick.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Adjust Safe Area and Brightness">
    Set your screen boundaries so that the minimap and ammo counters sit comfortably in your peripheral vision. Adjust the brightness slider until dark corners remain visible without washing out bright outdoor areas.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Audio for Footsteps">
    Switch your audio preset to Super Bass or Headphones. High-quality stereo audio is essential for tracking enemy footsteps in multiplayer or locating approaching zombies behind you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Sensitivity Limit">
Do not copy professional players' maximum sensitivity settings immediately. High sensitivity can severely degrade your medium-to-long-range accuracy with assault rifles like the M8A1 or AN-94.
</Callout>
